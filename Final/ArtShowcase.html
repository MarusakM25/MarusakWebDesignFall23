Where is the Majority of Vincent van Gogh's art?

The majority of Vincent van Gogh's art is in the Van Gogh Museum in Amsterdam, which houses the largest collection of his works, including more than 200 paintings, 500 drawings, 30 prints and 800 letters.

What are Some Other Museums with His Art?

Other museums that have significant collections of his art are the Kröller-Müller Museum in Otterlo, the Netherlands, the Musée d'Orsay in Paris, France, and the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York, USA. In total these 3 Museums have only 136 paintings and 186 drawings by Vincent Van Gogh. If you wanted to see many in one place you would need to go to Ampsterdam to go to the Van Gogh Museum.

Starry Night (1889) By: Vincent Van Gogh

A view of the night sky from his window at the asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, France. The painting is known for its swirling brushstrokes and vivid colors.

Located at the Museum of Modern Art New York City, USA

Self Portrait (1889) By: Vincent Van Gogh

One of the last self-portraits he made before his death in 1890. He painted himself with a bandaged ear and a palette and brushes, showing his identity and passion as a painter.

Located at the Musée d'Orsay in Paris, France

Sunflowers (1888-89) By: Vincent Van Gogh

Van Gogh created a series of five paintings of sunflowers in a vase, using only shades of yellow and green, to demonstrate his color mastery and to adorn his friend's bedroom.

Often located at the Van Gogh Museum in Amsterdam, But it often travels the world visiting museums.
