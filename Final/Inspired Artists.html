Inspired Artists Reason or How They Were Inspired
Pablo Picasso Van Gogh's influence on Pablo Picasso became recognizable in the mid-1960s. During this time, Picasso began painting self-portraits with many stylistic similarities to the work of Van Gogh, such as the rich brush strokes and striking colors
Pual Gauguin Van Gogh and Paul Gauguin lived together as roommates in Arles, France, in the late-1880s, which influenced their work during this time.
Henri Matisse The Australian painter John Russell introduced the young Matisse to Van Gogh's work.
Joan Miró Spanish artist, Joan Miró, was inspired by Van Gogh and another famous artist, Paul Cézanne1.
David Hockney Like Van Gogh, David Hockney is enamored with nature. Upon looking at some of Hockney's work, you may notice its similarities to Van Gogh's pieces.
Francis Bacon Irish-British artist Francis Bacon was particularly influenced by Van Gogh's The Painter on the Road to Tarascon.
André Derain Andre Derain was also taken by Van Gogh's work after seeing it on view at the Galerie Bernheim-Jeune in Paris.
